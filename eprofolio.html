eProfolio

Position: Lead Designer/Frontend Dev | Tech Used: React.js, CSS, Figma, Photoshop

The Project:

When applying for jobs, people need a way to bundle all of their work together into one folio. This process can be difficult, as people may find it difficult to know which work to include and how to format their folio correctly. The aim of this project was to provide a service that users can use to create their own ePortfolios easily. Users can use pre existing templates, or create their own in our workspace, which they can share for others to use. Our clients wanted a product that felt a lot like "Notion", a popular notetaking software. They also wanted a website that felt slick and easy to use.

My Work:

I played a vital part in the creation of the final product. In the team, I was the main designer and frontend developer. At the start of the project, I developed prototypes in Figma & Photoshop, which I would then send off to the client to receive feedback. After a couple of prototypes and designs, we arrived at a common theme among the website. When this stage was done, it was time to develop the product. At first, I had never used React - however, after a couple of weeks I had become proficient enough to create functional components and a functional product. After 12 weeks of development, I was able to create a fully functioning app that both worked well and felt good to use.
